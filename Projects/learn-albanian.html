Case Study 1 – Albanian Language Learning App

Learning Albanian can feel overwhelming, with few quality resources and ad-heavy apps. Many learners struggle to find structure, real audio, and encouragement along the way. I set out to design an app that makes the process simple, supportive, and motivating. AlbaniGo a language app that guides learners from beginner to advanced with confidence.

Research

Before designing the app, I set out to understand the main challenges of learning Albanian and to review the tools already available. I interviewed native speakers and explored existing apps and online communities to gain a well-rounded perspective.

I concluded that many learners feel overwhelmed and even anxious when starting Albanian. This reinforced the need for an app that feels gentle, visual, and supportive, especially for beginners.

From these conversations and reviews, I could see patterns in what learners need and where current tools fall short. This helped me define clear insights, which I summarized into four key findings:

  • Alphabet Support Learners struggle with 36 unique letters
  • Dialect Clarity Regional differences confuse beginners
  • Structured Learning Users want guided, ad-free systems
  • Natural Voices Robotic audio discourages progress, authentic sound builds confidence.

These findings became the foundation for our design decisions, guiding how we shaped the app experience.

Low fidelity design and prototype

Guerrilla test

Testing was conducted with 4 participants. Participants were asked:

  • Create a personal account
  • Choose your level
  • Start first lesson
  • Navigate back to lesson menu
  • View progress

Findings

Choosing a Level

Positive: Participants liked the clear beginner, intermediate, advanced options.

Issue: 1 participant wasn’t sure what “Beginner” included (alphabet? basic phrases?). Suggested adding short descriptions under each level.

First Design

Usability Test

Testing was conducted with 4 participants.

  • Online — 3 participants
  • Offline — 1 participant

Participants were asked to:

  • Scan the lessons list and open a lesson
  • View profile and achievements
  • Return to the home/lessons screen

Findings

  • Color red overuse: too intense; reduce saturation/opacity and reserve red for errors/alerts.
  • Icon style consistency: be selective, choosing minimal or abstract and keep one style set.
  • User profile polish: refine avatar size/border, spacing, and typography for a more polished look.

Conclusions on the project

  1. Small improvements matter. Even modest structural changes can quickly build user confidence.
  2. Simplicity reduces friction. Clear, straightforward flows lower cognitive load and make learning easier.
  3. Iterate continuously. Test → learn → refine. Rapid cycles lead to stronger solutions.

In the end, addressing even small UX issues can make a meaningful difference in people’s lives. By focusing on simplicity and intuitive design, this app supports learners effectively while creating an engaging and motivating experience.
